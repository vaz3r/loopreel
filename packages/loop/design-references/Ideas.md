### Part 1: New Template Themes (Art Directions)

If "The Paper of Record" is for *Authority & Investigation*, here are four other distinct brand voices we should create:

1. **The Globalist (Inspired by *Monocle* / *The Economist*)**
* **The Vibe:** International business, sophisticated travel, and diplomacy. Warm, optimistic, and highly structured.
* **Visuals:** Warm cream backgrounds, deep forest green or navy blue headers, and accents of mustard yellow. Typography uses geometric, precise sans-serifs (like Futura or Gill Sans) paired with highly legible body text.
* **Best For:** B2B consulting, macro-economics, travel, and premium lifestyle content.


2. **The Terminal (Inspired by *Bloomberg* / *Financial Times*)**
* **The Vibe:** High-speed data, market analysis, and quantitative truth.
* **Visuals:** Can be done in "Terminal Dark" (Pitch black with neon green/red data points) or "Salmon Print" (the iconic pink paper of the Financial Times). Heavy use of monospace fonts, ticker-tape borders, and dense, grid-locked data cells.
* **Best For:** FinTech, crypto, day-trading, and data-heavy analytics.


3. **The Curator (Inspired by *MoMA* / *Phaidon Art Books*)**
* **The Vibe:** Extreme luxury, quiet confidence, and avant-garde art.
* **Visuals:** Pure, blinding white backgrounds. Massive, unapologetic negative space. Typography is pushed to the extremes: either razor-thin, elegant serifs or heavy, brutalist sans-serifs, positioned asymmetrically.
* **Best For:** High-fashion, architecture, luxury real estate, and creative agencies.


4. **The Academic (Inspired by *Harvard Business Review* / *MIT Tech Review*)**
* **The Vibe:** Peer-reviewed, heavily researched, and deeply intellectual.
* **Visuals:** Crisp white and muted greys with a single, aggressive accent color (like a deep crimson or stark cobalt blue). Heavy use of structural lines, abstract diagrams, and block-quotes.
* **Best For:** Thought leadership, white-papers, scientific breakdowns, and executive coaching.

### Part 2: New Slide Types (Information Archetypes)

To make your LLM engine completely universal, it needs to be able to output more than just lists and quotes. We should introduce these new slide structures to the engine:

1. **The Q&A (Interview Format)**
* **Layout:** A conversational UI adapted for editorial. Distinct styling for the "Interviewer" (bold, accent color) and the "Subject" (indented, lighter text). Perfect for parsing podcast transcripts or interviews.


2. **The Quadrant (2x2 Matrix)**
* **Layout:** A literal 4-box grid (e.g., Eisenhower Matrix, BCG Matrix). The LLM can plot data into "High/Low" categories. Highly viral on LinkedIn and Twitter.


3. **Myth vs. Fact (Debunking)**
* **Layout:** A high-contrast binary slide. Top half is the "Myth" (muted, crossed out, or red), bottom half is the "Fact" (highlighted, bold, or green).


4. **The Case Study (3-Part Framework)**
* **Layout:** A strictly divided slide showing: *The Challenge*, *The Action*, and *The Result*. Essential for B2B marketing and agency portfolios.


5. **The Resource Stack (Tool Grid)**
* **Layout:** A 4-to-6 box grid designed to list tools, books, or frameworks with a title and a 1-sentence description per box. (e.g., "Top 4 AI Tools for X").


6. **The Manifesto (Typographic Poster)**
* **Layout:** A single, massive block of text that fills the entire 1080x1350 canvas edge-to-edge. Used for powerful, disruptive statements that require the reader to stop scrolling.
