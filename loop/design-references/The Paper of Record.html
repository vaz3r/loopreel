Analysis: The Opt-Out

There is a growing movement among the technological elite to entirely disconnect their children from the very systems they built. In Silicon Valley, nanny contracts now routinely include strict "no-screen" clauses. This creates a fascinating paradox: the architects of the attention economy treat their own product like a hazardous material. As this awareness trickles down to the middle class, we are seeing the first widespread adoption of "dumb phones" and timed router lockouts. The future of luxury may no longer be defined by what you have access to, but by what you have the privilege to ignore.
